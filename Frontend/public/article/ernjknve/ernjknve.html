DIP Tinting

Process principle

Dip tinting is a common and cheap method used for coloration of lenses.

Here's a simplified explanation of the process:

Cleaning:

Dip tinting in main baths: to give the right color to lenses.

Color inspection: to check the tint and the intensity of the tint.

Cleaning: to remove the dyes remaining on the lens.

Curing: to fix the dyes.

 

  1. Lens Cleaning: Before dipping, the lenses are carefully cleaned and prepared to ensure that the tint adheres evenly and effectively. Its also to remove particles and dust before tinting.
  2. Dye Solution: A dye solution is prepared, containing the desired colorant. This solution can be a specific dye or a combination of dyes, depending on the desired tint color.
  3. Dipping Process: The lenses are dipped into the dye solution. The duration of the dip, along with factors like temperature and concentration of the dye solution, can influence the intensity of the tint. This process allows the dye to penetrate the lens material.
  4. Color Inspection: The tinted lenses are evaluated to check the tint and the intensity of the tint. Any defects or inconsistencies may be addressed at this stage.
  5. Rinsing and Drying: After the desired tint is achieved, the lenses are carefully rinsed to remove any excess dye solution. The lenses are then dried thoroughly.
  6. Curing: to fix the dyes.

 

Dip tinting is a versatile process that allows for a range of tint colors and intensities. It is commonly used for sunglasses and fashion eyewear to provide both aesthetic appeal and functional benefits, such as reducing glare or enhancing contrast. The process is cost-effective and can be easily adapted for different lens materials and designs

 

 

1.Principle of surface tinting

1.1 Tinting by immersion

We know that the lenses are not tinted in the mass, but on the surface.

See how the color is carried out...

 

The tinting principle is the immersion of the lens in a water bath containing disperse dyes.

                 

        Thus, the tinting is achieved by the migration of dyes

in the area of the substrate, at a very small depth.

For a color class 3, the penetration depth is about 15 to 20 µm on organic substrates.

1.2 Main baths

 

For the most common colors, there are different main baths available in the workshop
These baths are named as colors.

It was determined that the optimum temperature for the process is 94 ° C - each bath is maintained at this temperature.

 

Baths can be inert - like the “mini-coloring” station.

         

 

or agitated - e.g. Haake baths, which are equipped with a circulation system.

 

As for those of TFA 3-10 machines, they are agitated using a magnetic stirrer.

 

Agitated baths have some benefits:

        Homogeneity of the resulting color

        Tint stability

        More rapid tinting

1.3 Process for the uniform colors

  1.       For the uniform tinting, we first check the cosmetic condition of the lens, and clean it with alcohol.
     
  2.       Then it is positioned in a basket
    Some baskets may contain up to 10 lenses, which can handle multiple orders in the same color at the same time.

     

  1.       Then we plunge integrally the lenses in the convenient bath.
    The immersion time depends on different parameters such as the intensity of the color to be obtained, the nature of the substrate, and its surface hardness.
    As the case may be from several seconds to over an hour ...

 

1.4 Process for gradient colors

In the case of gradient colors, it is first necessary to materialize the mounting axis of the lens, which will generally also be the gradient axis.


This axis is determined by pointing the micro-circles for progressive lenses.
               or with a lensometer for other types of lenses.

                                                                 


With the help of a drawing map, we engrave each lens on 3 positions, by light lines in the saw - one at each end of the axis - and a double line to the point located at the bottom side of the mounted lens.

                


Once the notches are made, we clean the lenses with alcohol, and each pair is positioned in a basket, the double notch at the top.

                                

The equipment used for the gradient tinting makes an up and down vertical movement for a specified time.

 

 

So the bottom of the lens is submerged

longer and the color is darker.


There are different equipment, such as new TFL programmable machines, and agitated baths have an automatic control level.

 

Dans tous les cas, lorsque le temps prévu est écoulé, on sort le panier.
In all cases, when the scheduled time is reached, we remove the basket.
Then we rinsed the lenses carefully.
Note that if the control can not be performed immediately, the lenses should be kept in water.


We must control the color by comparing each lens - either to the standard, or to a control lens, or another lens of a mismatched pair.
